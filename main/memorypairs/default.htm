Memory Pairs is a simple game requiring you to concentrate and use your memory.

Underneath each of the 36 covers is an image. There are 18 pairs of images, and the idea is to find each of the pairs.



You can reveal any two images by pressing on one of the covers, which displays the first image, then pressing on the second cover. After the second image has been displayed, if the two images are the same then the covers are left off and you can then move on to investigating some other images. If, instead, the two images are different, both the images are then covered up again. Just remember where the images are and find the pairs.

Two numbers are shown, one either side of the New button:
  • the one on the left indicates how many pairs have still to be found;
  • the one on the right indicates the number of avoidable errors made, i.e. when the second cover pressed a) has been pressed before and b) does not match the first one.
Your objective is to try to minimize the number of avoidable errors made (indicated by the number on the right) before revealing all pairs.

New Game

Just press on the New button at any time to start a new game with, of course, a different layout of the hidden images.
